    15 best beer subscriptions UK for 2023, for craft beer, ale and lager enthusiasts

    07 Oct 2024, 00:00

    THERE’S nothing like a cold beer after a long day at work and by signing up to a beer club you make sure you never end up crying into your lemonade at the fact you didn’t make it to the shops for your new supply of ale.

    The best beer subscriptions send you a good selection of the beers you like, perhaps different styles, perhaps from different countries – there are so many options you can go with what’s right for you.

    No two subscriptions are the same either, with some specialising in certain types of craft beer nationwide - or globally.

    Others come with magazine subscriptions included, or snacks, food pairing ideas and more. The one thing they have in common? Ale subscriptions deliver good beer, at competitive prices straight to your door - often with free delivery, too.

    New to craft beer, or looking to expand your palate? No sweat. The best beer subscriptions invite you to be adventurous, if you so wish, and can deliver a range of craft beers from unheard of breweries - including beer styles like IPAs, pale ales, lagers, pilsners, witbier, stouts, porters, goses, lambics, sours, and much, much more.

    If the option to be adventurous isn’t quite right for you, then you can tailor your craft beer subscription to suit your taste buds and pocket. One thing is for sure, though - you’ll never get bored, and reach for the cheap, supermarket own tinnies again.

    Not a big drinker or looking to cut down on your beer consumption? No problem - there are alcohol-free beer subscriptions on offer, too.

    We’ve rounded up the most exciting, best beer subscriptions to suit you and your drinking - or spending - habits. We’d recommend making space in your cupboard, first, though.

    Best beer subscriptions at a glance:

    Beavertown

    If you like Beavertown, you'll love their monthly subscription options.

    By subscribing, not only will you get your favourite beers from the cult brand delivered to your door each month, but you can also make savings.

    If you get a monthly subscription of 12 cans, you'll save £3 on each crate and if you up the order to 24 cans a month, you'll save £5 per crate.

    That's good value! Although you do have to sign up for a minimum of two months to get the discount.

    Abel & Cole

    Organic produce supplier Abel & Cole now offers a British beer subscription so you can add your favourite IPA, bitters and lagers to your next food delivery.

    You’ll receive 12 craft beers from across the UK, made up of six permanent fixtures and six guest ales that change every fortnight. All beers included in the subscription case are organic.

    Opt for delivery every 1, 2, 3, 4, or 8 weeks depending on what suits you best. There’s no minimum commitment and you can cancel at any time.

    Bottles/cans: 12 subscription: 1 - 8 weeks minimum commitment: no

    Beer52

    • Craft Beer Discovery Club, from £27 per month at Beer52 - buy here

    Beer52 is a fantastic way to travel the world of beer, without having to leave your home.

    Choose from a Mixed case - this box includes up to three stouts, porters, or other types of dark beer alongside your regular beers - or a Light case boasting pale ales, IPAs, lagers and so forth, and bring the variety of beer to your door.

    Starting from £27 per month, you can expect to receive eight different beers from a variety of breweries, in a huge range of styles and flavours, as well as a snack and Beer52's award-winning magazine, Ferment.

    Billed as the UK's largest craft beer discovery club, you can sup beer from as far as Australia, America, Scandinavia, and more - finding new favourites and discovering eclectic beers you otherwise would never have come across.

    For £27, you can get eight beers per month, or it's £32 for 10 beers and £37 for 12 beers - with all prices including delivery.

    Bottles/cans: 8 – 12 subscription: monthly minimum commitment: no

    PerfectDraft

    • PerfectDraft beer subscription, around £90 per month – buy here 

    PerfectDraft (formerly Beer Hawk) now offers kegs on subscription, but you will need the PerfectDraft Machine or the PerfectDraft Pro to drink it.

    Chose three kegs from nine options including Leffe Blonde, Jupiler and Hoegaarden and save 15% on the regular price. Each keg holds six litres of beer so you’re not likely to run out.

    Price varies depending on what beers you choose, but it’s around £90 per month. There’s no minimum subscription and you can cancel at any time.

    Read our review of the PerfectDraft Pro. 

    Bottles/cans: 3 kegs subscription: monthly minimum commitment: no

    Beerhunter

    Beerhunter offers a number of different subscriptions, including German Lagers, British craft beers and world beers, so no matter what your preference you’ll find something to satisfy your thirst. There’s even an alcohol-free subscription option.

    Choose from a monthly subscription or buy a three-month subscription in advance – makes a great gift. There’s no minimum time commitment and you can cancel at any time.

    With your first order, you’ll also receive a Beerhunter bottle opener and schooner glass with which to toast your new beer relationship.

    Bottles/cans: 6 – 12 subscription: monthly minimum commitment: no

    Dry Drinker

    • Subscription Box, £22 per month for six months from Dry Drinker - buy here

    Cutting down on alcohol or want to stick to the light stuff mid-week? Don't miss out on the taste of beer with Dry Drinker's subscription for just £22 per month - and there's currently 25% off your first box. Each month you receive eight of the best alcohol-free craft beers of the moment, selected by Dry Drinker's crew - apparently, the brand only sends beers they'd drink themselves, too.

    Choose between light beers or a mixture of light and dark, depending on your preference.

    For your first box, you will also receive a specially branded glass to help you sip and savour your way to low-and-no heaven.

    For more alcohol-free options, check out our round-up of the best non-alcoholic drinks.

    Bottles/cans: 8 subscription: monthly minimum commitment: 3 0r 6 months

    Beerwulf

    • Pick 'N' Mix subscription, from £71.35 per month for eight 2L kegs at Beerwulf - buy here

    If you're the lucky owner of a SUB craft beer dispenser (we are, and we love it) - you'll be happy to hear that the brand that it calls home, Beerwulf, boasts a selection of Keg SUBscription plans to help you save money each month while stocking up on your favourite beer kegs.

    There are over 40 choices of kegged beer to choose from, including Birra Moretti, Heineken, Amstel and more - with subscriptions from Beerwulf available both monthly and bi-monthly, saving you up to £9 on every shipment. The minimum commitment is six months.

    Head on over to read our SUB review, or check out our updated round-up of the best beer dispensers on the market right now.

    Bottles/cans: 8 x 2L kegs subscription: monthly or bi-monthly minimum commitment: 6 months

    Great Newsome Brewery Great Beer Club

    • Great Newsome Brewery Great Beer Club, from £68 for two months – buy here 

    How about a bottle of Pricky Back Otchan or award-winning Frothingham Best to quench your thirst? They’re just two of the beers from Great Newsome Brewery in Hull – and you can savour others just like this when you sign up for their Great Beer Club.

    The minimum subscription is two months, paid in advance, which works out to £34 per month for 12 bottles. Other options include 3, 6 and 12-month subscriptions.

    Great Newsome Brewery was founded in 2007 by Matthew Hodgson whose family has farmed in East Yorkshire for generations. They use homegrown malting barley, their own strain of yeast and Yorkshire water in their beers, the names of which reflect the local dialect, such as Stoggy (wood pigeon) and Sleck Dust (how you quench your thirst after a hard day’s work).

    Bottles/cans: 12 subscription: monthly minimum commitment: 2 months

    Brew York Beer Club

    • Brew York Beer Club, from £23 per month – buy here 

    There are three subscription levels at Brew York Beer Club: Explorer, Member and Gold Member. Choose Explorer you receive six beers, with Member you get nine and with Gold Member it’s 12.

    With names like Juice Forsyth, Calmer Chameleon, Schooner Matata and Lo Rider the beer sounds fun as well as refreshing.

    The company, which was set up in 2016, brews all its beer in York in their city centre brewhouse and along the road in Osbaldwick.

    The ethos of founders Wayne Smith and Lee Grabham is to brew the beer they like to drink and there are always around 20 different beers on the go, with new beers released regularly.

    If you don’t want to wait for your subscription then you can always buy a round in Brew York’s tap rooms in York, Pocklington and Leeds.

    Bottles/cans: 6 – 12 subscription: monthly minimum commitment: no

    Flavourly

    • Flavourly Beer Club, £39.95 per month - buy here

    Whether you fancy a beer subscription monthly, bi-monthly or quarterly, Flavourly's option works out at less than £1.70 a beer.

    With each box, you can stock up your fridge with 24 high-quality small-batch beers from a variety of cracking independent microbreweries, curated by the expert in-house team of beer lovers over at Flavourly.

    Get to know each brewery's story, too, thanks to the brand's magazine which is included in every box.

    With a Flavourly subscription, you can expect to receive a minimum of eight different varieties per month, and no more than three cans of any single beer - ideal for those wishing to branch out from their trusty supermarket go-to's.

    There's no contract or commitment, either, and if you need to take a break, simply press pause or extend your subscription.

    Bottles/cans: 24 subscription: monthly minimum commitment: no

    Brewgooder

    • Original Core Range Mixed Pack, from £22 every 30 days at Brewgooder - buy here

    Certified B-Corp Brewgooder has been brewing beer to empower others for around five years after its founder dedicated his life to fighting poverty.

    It's the first ever Scottish brewery to be accredited charity status, and a portion of every beer sold contributes to helping fund clean water projects - impacting over 150,000 lives.

    Now, you can choose to drink good beer that does well with Brewgooder's range of beer subscription boxes - including our favourite, the Original Core Range Mixed Pack.

    Including its core range of beers, Session IPA, Tropical Pale and Lager, choose to receive a selection of tinnies from £22 every seven, 14, 30 or 60 days.

    You can choose how many you receive too, depending on your thirst (or fridge space!) including a 12-pack, 24-pack or 48-pack - ideal for keeping guests happy and nicely watered at your next summer party.

    Plus, with each 12-pack, you'll help provide 396 litres of clean water to those who need it most. We'll raise a glass to that.

    Bottles/cans: 12 – 48 subscription: 7, 14, 30 or 60 days minimum commitment: no

    Brewser

    • Brewser craft beer subscription box, £38 per month - buy here

    Is there a brewery's beers you've always fancied getting your hands on but can't, for love nor money, get your hands on some? Or perhaps, you wouldn't know what to order? Have no fear, leave it up to Brewser.

    With no hassle or commitments, meaning you can take a break, edit or cancel any time - a Brewser subscription offers both freedom and variety.

    Every month, for £38, choose an independent brewery from its list of over 120 breweries nationwide you'd like to discover, and Brewser will do the rest. Or, if you can't bear to make a decision, choose its surprise box!

    You can also choose your preferred box style, too, from a list including dark boxes, gluten-free boxes, vegan ones, mixed, sours, low & no, IPAS/pales and more.

    For every box delivered, Brewser will also plant a tree and it works with each brewery to minimise environmental impact with attempts to make sure as much packagaing as possible is recyclable - all as part of its efforts to become a fully carbon neutral business.

    Bottles/cans: number varies subscription: monthly minimum commitment: no

    Hop Burns and Black

    • All Killer No Filler beer subscription box, from £49.95 per month - buy here

    For under £50 per month, you can get your grubby mitts on an All Killer No Filler beer subscription from Hop Burns and Black - and it does exactly as it says on the tin (...or box).

    Each month you will find 10 of the very best beers on the market right now, including rare international beers, exclusives, small-batch collabs and more of the most sought-after beers in the nation.

    Every box comes with tasting notes for each beer, reviews, and even suggested food pairings and recipes from Hop Burns and Black's in-house beer and food writers.

    Choosing a subscription means you save, on average, around 20% of what each beer would cost - helping you try out new and exciting beers, for less.

    And, if you find yourself with too much - or simply not enough - beer in your fridge, you can press pause and edit or cancel your subscription at any time.

    Bottles/cans: 10 subscription: monthly minimum commitment: no

    Clapton Craft

    • Clapton Craft Selects Subscription, from £55 every four weeks - buy here

    London-based Clapton Craft is renowned for its stellar selection of beers and wines, and its craft beer subscription box is no exception.

    Dispatched every fortnight, or monthly (on Wednesdays) each box brings you 12 of the freshest, hottest brews from London, the UK and across the globe.

    You can expect 12 different breweries in each box, including small-batch rare beers, the shop's highlights, collabs or even the beers that don't even make it to the shelves as they're so sought after.

    Choose from the brand's Mixed Bangers selections or its Hop Drop - either way, the best clear space in your fridge now.

    Bottles/cans: 12 subscription: fortnightly or monthly minimum commitment: no

    Best of British Beer

    • Craft Beer Club, £24 per month from Best of British Beer - buy here

    British craft beer is rivalling the rest of the world with its selection, variety and unrivalled taste - now, the Best of British Beer is treating you to boxes of the good stuff with its Craft Beer Club.

    For £24 per month, you can have six of the best small-batch, British beers delivered straight to your door, mostly in 500ml bottles but there will be the occasional 440ml can.

    Each case will also contain a pub quiz to be the star of your next summer party, and tasting notes - your first box will contain a glass, too.

    Your beer will change every month so you could get to try up to 72 different beers over the course of the year.

    There's also an option to upsize and receive 12 bottles each month.

    Alternatively, if you prefer lager you can opt for the craft lager club, which starts at £23.50 per month for six bottles/cans. 

    Talk about being adventurous!

    Bottles/cans: 6 – 12 subscription: monthly minimum commitment: no

    Where to buy beer subscriptions in the UK

    You’ll find lots of beer subscriptions online – both from independent breweries and from companies that only sell subscriptions.

    It’s worth doing a search for the type of beer you’re interested in and browsing the many results so that you find the best beer subscription for your needs. Some for example offer bar snacks to go with your ale, while others offer discounted tickets to events.

    How much do beer subscriptions cost?

    The cost of beer subscriptions varies depending on how many bottles or cans your monthly subscription offers.

    The price also varies between companies as the products are different, as well as the sizes, frequency of delivery and beer types. Some companies offer different tiers of subscription also, so it’s best to check the small print of the subscription you’re interested in.

    For our roundup prices start at £22 per month, but expect to pay an average of around £30 plus. Keg subscriptions will obviously cost more because of the volume of ale involved.

    What is the most popular beer subscription in the UK

    Well that’s a difficult one to answer. Real ale drinkers will choose an ale subscription while lager lovers will opt for a lager subscription, so it really depends on what you like to drink.
