    Arsenal predicted line-up with Emery facing number ten dilemma, Mkhitaryan doubtful but Lacazette ba

    UNAI EMERY could welcome back Alexandre Lacazette to fire Arsenal into the Europa League quarter-finals - but Henrikh Mkhitaryan may be unavailable. The French striker was supposed to be banned for three games after his first-leg red card against BATE Borisov but had his suspension reduced.

    03 Sep 2024, 00:00

    UNAI EMERY could welcome back Alexandre Lacazette to fire Arsenal into the Europa League quarter-finals - but Henrikh Mkhitaryan may be unavailable.

    The French striker was supposed to be banned for three games after his first-leg red card against BATE Borisov but had his suspension reduced.

    Arsenal face an uphill task to make it through to the final eight after a shocking performance in France last week saw them beaten 3-1.

    And as the race for a top-four finish looks set to go down to the wire, the Europa League may prove to be crucial in qualifying for next season's Champions League.

    Emery continues to switch between a back three and a back four while a lack of defensive options could be problematic once again.

    Sokratis' red card in the first leg means he is suspended for the clash at the Emirates tomorrow.

    So Emery may opt to bring Shkodran Mustafi into a back three to replace the Greek star after the clean sheet against Manchester United on Sunday.

    Ainsley Maitland-Niles and Sead Kolasinac could then keep their places at wing-back.

    Another option, though could see Mustafi keep his place at right-back - he played in the role against Rennes last week - with Koscielny and Nacho Monreal in the middle and Kolasinac at left-back.

    Who partners Xhaka?

    Granit Xhaka is one of the first names on the team sheet at the moment and his goal against United will have done him the world of good - even if David De Gea should have done better.

    But there is an ongoing debate as to who should start alongside the Swiss star.

    Lucas Torreira started the season brilliantly and chipped in with a few crucial goals but Matteo Guendouzi is in fine form.

    It will be interesting to see who gets the nod from Emery as it is unlikely all three would start in a game Arsenal must score at least twice in.

    The No10 Dilemma

    Mesut Ozil started in the defeat at Rennes but then had one of his better games of the season when he featured at the Emirates against Ole Gunnar Solskjaer's side.

    But it seems impossible to predict just how Emery will use the No10 role.

    Ozil is of course the most-talented Gunners player on his day - but will the boss be able to rely on him when he needs to step up and deliver?

    Aaron Ramsey is one option while Henrikh Mkhitaryan can also play in there.

    However, the Armenian faces a late fitness test ahead of the game to check a mild back strain.

    Aubameyang? Lacazette? Both?

    A week ago, Emery did not really have a decision to make - Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang had to start up front.

    That is because Alexandre Lacazette was serving the second of his three-game suspension following his red card in the first leg against BATE Borisov in the last round.

    But Uefa have reduced the ban and he is now available again - and just in time with Arsenal knowing victory by at least two goals is the only option.

    Aubameyang and Lacazette started together in attack on Sunday and are expected to do the same again at the Emirates tomorrow but the Gunners boss may opt to use one out wide or even as a super sub.
