    Who is Lauren Jeska? As the fell runner admits Ralph Knibbs stabbing here's what we know

    A FORMER athlete has admitted trying to kill a British Athletics official today. Lauren Jeska pleaded guilty to the attempted murder of Ralph Knibbs and assaults on two other men who stepped in to help him.

    18 Mar 2024, 00:00

    A FORMER athlete has admitted trying to kill a British Athletics official today.

    Lauren Jeska pleaded guilty to the attempted murder of Ralph Knibbs and assaults on two other men who stepped in to help him.

    The 41-year-old, of Machynlleth, Powys, tried to kill former rugby player Ralph by stabbing him at the Alexander Stadium in Birmingham this March.

    Jeska also admitted two charges of having a knife in a public place during a video-link appearance at Birmingham Crown Court.

    She first appeared in court charged with the crimes in May, but the case was adjourned so a psychiatric evaluation could be carried out.

    But who exactly is Lauren Jeska? And why did she try to murder an athletics official?

    We tell you what we know so far about the fallen star…

    Profile

    Lauren Jeska is a former fell running champion from Wales.

    She was listed as being a member of the Yorkshire-based Todmorden Harriers Running Club on the group’s website.

    It says she was the women’s 2010, 2011 and 2012 English Fell Running Champion and won the British Championship in 2012.

    Crime

    Police and paramedics rushed to the Alexander Stadium in Birmingham, at 10.30am on March 23 to reports of a multiple stabbing.

    When cops searched Jeska they found she was carrying a 12cm kitchen knife, a 13cm kitchen knife and a bread knife.

    Former England rugby star Mr Knibbs, the head of human resources and welfare for UK Athletics, was rushed to hospital.

    Jeska was charged with attempted murder of Mr Knibbs, which she today admitted.

    She was also accused of wounding Tim Begley and Kevan Taylor with intent when they stepped in to stop her.

    But she denied these charges, admitting instead to assault occasioning actual bodily harm.

    She also admitted two charges of carrying kitchen knives, but denies one of carrying a bread knife.

    Jeska had the bread knife in her bag throughout the attack, but did not take it out.

    Mr Knibbs represented England at under-23 level, playing 436 times for Bristol Rugby Football Club and scoring 123 tries during his career spanning the 1970s and 1980s.

    He famously turned down the chance to go on tour with England to South Africa in 1984 because he opposed apartheid.

    He also ruled himself out of the 1988 England tour of Australia and Fiji due to work commitments, making him one of the only players to ever turn down England twice.

    Jeska’s motives for the attack have not yet come to light.

    But the former running star has been diagnosed with autism since the incident.

    She will now remain in custody until she is sentenced on 15 November.
