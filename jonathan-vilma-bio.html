    Jonathan Vilma: Early Life & Family

    Starting, Jonathan Polynice Vilma was born to his parents, Fritz Vilma and Nelly Vilma, on April 16, 1982, in Coral Gables, Florida.

    Furthermore, Jonathan’s parents were originally from Haiti before immigrating to the U.S. Besides, the Florida native has a sister, Alice Vilma, with whom he grew up.

    But, despite hours of searching, we still could not find any details regarding his childhood.

    But, we did find out that he attended the G.W. Carver Middle School and then Coral Gables High School, where he earned a name for himself playing for his high school football team.

    After that, Vilma enrolled at the University of Miami after receiving a scholarship at the University of Miami.

    Subsequently, the Florida native played for the Miami Hurricanes football team from 2000 to 2003, impressing many NFL scouts on the way.

    In fact, by the time he finished his four-year stint, Vilma was a three-time Academic All-Big East Conference.

    Thus, at the end of 2003, Jonathan graduated from the University of Miami with a finance degree from the School of Business Administration.

    Jonathan Vilma: Career & Stats

    Jonathan started his professional career with the New York Jets when they selected him 12th overall in the 2004 NFL Draft. Having been one of the top picks, expectations were high for the Florida native to perform.

    Subsequently, he rose to the expectations as he won the NFL Defensive Rookie of the Year. Besides, Vilma finished the campaign with 107 tackles, three interceptions, and two sacks.

    Thus, having just had a great year, the pressure was on Jonathan to perform even better during his second season in the NFL.

    And that is precisely what the 41-year-old did as he led the NFL in tackles with 169. Furthermore, Vilma also made his first appearance at Pro Bowl in 2006.

    Thereafter, the Florida native had another commendable season with the Jets compiling stats of 114 tackles, one fumble recovery, a forced fumble, and an interception.

    However, Vilma’s fourth year with the franchise turned out to be a nightmare. The reason being, he suffered a season-ending knee injury in the week seven game against the Cincinnati Bengals.

    Thus, a combination of injury and short duration left in the contract led the Jets to trade the 6 feet 1 linebacker to the New Orleans Saints for a fourth-round draft pick in the 2008 NFL Draft and another 2009 NFL Draft.

    New Orleans Saints

    Having just been traded to the Saints, everyone expected Jonathan to take time to adapt. However, he did the opposite as he was the only positive in a weak defensive unit, which conceded many touchdowns.

    Nonetheless, the 41-year-old ended his first season with the saints with 132 tackles and one sack in 16 games.

    Moving on, the following year, no one would have expected the New Orleans Saints to reach the Super Bowl, let alone win it.

    But that is what happened as Vilma led his team to the Super Bowl XLIV after making some vital plays that helped them beat the Indianapolis Colts 31-17.

    Added to that, Jonathan was also selected for his second Pro Bowl appearance.

    After that, Vilma had a pretty good 2010 season, where he led the team in tackles, and then had a disastrous 2011 due to a knee injury.

    However, no one predicted the storm that New Orleans Saints and Vilma would have to face in the 2012 season.
