    How Old Is Flames Exec?

    Chris Snow has been in Wikipedia searches ever since the recent headline about his death. Chris Snow had been living with ALS since 2019. Unfortunately, Chris lost his life to the disease on Saturday.

    29 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Chris Snow has been in Wikipedia searches ever since the recent headline about his death. Chris Snow had been living with ALS since 2019. Unfortunately, Chris lost his life to the disease on Saturday.

    Following a series of doctor’s appointments and tests, Snow, a father of two, was diagnosed with ALS in June 2019.

    He had lost his father six months before his diagnosis, as well as two uncles and a cousin to the same disease. 

    Chris Snow, born on August 18, 1981, was the assistant general manager of the Calgary Flames of the National Hockey League (NHL).

    Born in Melrose, Massachusetts, Snow had journalism and policy studies degrees from Syracuse University. 

    Snow had been part of the Calgary Flames for a long time. The 42-year-old started his journey with the Flames in 2011 as the director of hockey analysis.

    After 13 years in the NHL, in 2019, the Calgary Flames appointed Snow as their Assistant General Manager. 

    His primary role included working closely with the general manager, Brad Treliving, and analyzing data to inform decision-making in all hockey-related operations.

    Who is Chris Snow? Wikipedia And Age

    Ever since the announcement of Snow battling with the life-threatening disease, his Wikipedia searches had gone exponentially up. However, the Flames executive didn’t have a Wikipedia page.

    He was quite popular within the Flames fanbase, as the exec had served the Flames for over a decade.

    As the director of hockey analysis, Snow started a hockey research and development department that was responsible for supporting different aspects of the team’s operation.

    Furthermore, his research and development department made significant contributions to player identification, player performance, and game strategy.

    Moreover, the exec for the Flames was regularly involved in player contract research as well as negotiation with players.

    Before joining the Flames in 2011, Snow was director of hockey operations for the Minnesota Wild between 2006 and 2010.

    Additionally, Snow worked as a writer covering the Minnesota Wild for the Minneapolis Star Tribune and the Boston Red Sox for The Boston Globe before starting in team management.

    Sadly, Snow passed away due to his illness, leaving behind a legacy of dedication and innovation within the Flames organization.

    Snow met his wife, Kelsie, while working as a sportswriter for The Boston Globe. After dating for a while, the couple tied the knot in 2007.

    Snow and his wife, Kelsie, had two children, Cohen and Willa.

    Chris Snow Wikipedia And Age | Battle With ALS

    When he was diagnosed with Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis, or ALS, in 2019, the doctors had given Snow no more than a year. Three years after that diagnosis, Snow was still alive and continued his battle with ALS.

    However, after a cardiac arrest on September 27, 2023, the Flames exec suffered a catastrophic brain injury.

    Kelsie Snow, the Flames exec, took to X and shared, “With a shattered heart, I’ve come to share that yesterday Chris became unresponsive and went into cardiac arrest.”

    She further added that the doctors were able to get his heartbeat back, but scans showed that he suffered a catastrophic brain injury due to lack of oxygen.

    Moreover, she mentioned that the doctors did not expect him to wake up from this injury.

    Unfortunately, the disease ran in the family, as Snow had lost his father, two uncles, and a cousin to the disease.

    Despite being diagnosed with the deadly disease, the assistant GM continued his work with the Flames.

    Following the use of an experimental drug, he beat the initial prognosis of 1 year as the medicine was successful in silencing the mutated gene.

    However, the disease affected Snow’s muscles and his ability to speak and eat.

    Following the recent news about his brain injury, the Calgary Flames posted on X how important Snow had been for their organization and how his fight against the disease had inspired everyone in the organization.

    They expressed their heartfelt gratitude and sent thoughts and prayers to Kelsie, Cohen, and Willa in this time of difficulty.

    Death

    Calgary Flames assistant general manager Chris Snow has sadly passed away at the age of 42 after a lengthy public battle with Lou Gehrig’s disease (Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis, or ALS). The Flames confirmed his death on Saturday night.

    In a statement, the Flames expressed their condolences, saying, “We, along with the entire hockey community, are mourning the passing of Chris Snow.

    Even while battling ALS, Chris dedicated his life to helping others, and he changed the lives of so many.”

    Kelsie Snow, his wife, shared the heartbreaking news on social media, revealing that her husband had gone into cardiac arrest on Tuesday due to a catastrophic brain injury caused by a lack of oxygen. Doctors had not expected him to wake up from this devastating injury.

    Chris Snow’s courageous battle with ALS and his contributions to the hockey world will be remembered and celebrated by many.

    His passing is a significant loss to the Flames organization and the broader hockey community.
