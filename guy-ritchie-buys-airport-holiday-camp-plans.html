    Guy Ritchie buys aerodrome as part of his plans to build holiday camp for rich and famous

    07 Jun 2024, 00:00

    FILMMAKER Guy Ritchie has bought an aerodrome as part of his plans to build a holiday camp for the rich and famous.

    He has snapped up picturesque Compton Abbas Airfield next to his sprawling 1,200-acre Ashcombe estate on the Dorset-Wiltshire border.

    In a post on the airfield’s website, Ritchie says: “Improvements to the buildings and infrastructure will be made in a sympathetic manner.”

    Ritchie, 54, is already locked in a planning war with the local parish council over plans to build 11 luxury cabins on his estate.

    Eric Adams, a councillor, said in a submission to the planning authority: “It is a new housing estate, isn’t it? It is a huge application.

    “We want the council to turn the application down, it was unanimous. This is an area of outstanding beauty.”

    READ MORE ON GUY RITCHIE

    If he gets the go-ahead, guests will be able to go shooting, sample beer from an on-site brewery, and stay in pioneer-style cabins.

    They will be kitted out in the utmost luxury with copper taps, leather sofas and insulated wooden floor.

    Ritchie bought the country pile with ex-wife Madonna in 2001 before landing it as part of a £50million divorce settlement seven years later.

    The estate’s Georgian manor house was previously the home of renowned photographer Cecil Beaton.

    Madonna said of Ashcombe: “It has something very mystical about it…in the summertime it’s the most beautiful place in the world.”

    Ritchie now lives on the estate with second wife, Jacqui Ainsley and their children.

    He was granted permission to build two lakes on the estate in 2018 and in 2019 planning chiefs agreed he could provide B&B for wealthy guests on his land.
