    Heidi Watney's Bio (Age): Born To The Sports Family

    In Fresno, California, Heidi was born to father Lon Watney and mother Mary Watney on 19 May 1981 and grew up as one of the five siblings. Heidi attended the Clovis West High School, and later graduated from the University of San Diego with an academic scholarship in 2003.

    Heidi's love for sports is derived from her family; Watney family was the family immersed to their nose in sports. Heidi's father, Lon, was a golf coach for Fresco State men's golf team for 35 years before quitting in 2013. Lon has coached many professional golfers throughout his career, including his nephew and Heidi's cousin, Nick Watney.

    Apart from her father, Heidi's elder sister is also a sportsperson and coaches women's volleyball team for the Fresco Pacific University.

    Brother's Death Left Her Distraught

    Growing up, Heidi was very close to one of her brother, Dusty who was born with Prader Willi Syndrome. When Dusty passed away in April 2018, she was left distraught. However, her large family helped her smother the loss of her brother.

    Salary Earned As SportsCaster

    During her early days, Heidi worked as a host and sports anchor for KMPH-TV and KFIG-AM radio in Fresno, California. She also worked for KUSI News as a sports reporter and assistant producer located in San Diego.

    She worked with NESN for three years until she joined MLB Network. She covers special events with the network and serves as a reporter at the World series, Postseason and All-Star Game.
