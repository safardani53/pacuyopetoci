    Beloved grocery store abruptly closes after 30 years as owner posts heartbreaking note on door

    05 Jun 2024, 00:00

    AFTER three decades of service, a beloved grocery store abruptly closed as the owner left a heartbreaking note posted on the front door.

    Keith Langston, who owns Two Rivers Grocery and Market in Big Sandy, Texas wrote that he had a "mix of emotions" as he wrote the letter to his loyal customers.

    "After 30 remarkable years, it is with both a heavy heart and a sense of gratitude that I must announce Two Rivers Grocery and Market will be closing its doors.

    "This decision was not made lightly, and we bid farewell to an era that has been filled with countless memories, cherished relationships, and unwavering support. It has been an incredible journey, and I am sincerely grateful for the dedication and support you have shown throughout this time."

    Langston wrote that the mission of the store was to provide quality groceries and exceptional customer service while also fostering a warm, welcoming environment for everyone who walked through the front doors.

    He thanked all the customers the store served for their "unwavering support."

    "We have witnessed moments of joy, celebrations, and sometimes even sorrow. From the friendly conversations at the checkout lines to the relationships that blossomed over the years, your patronage has meant more to us than words can express," Langston wrote.

    "Your faith in our store and the connections we have formed go far beyond business transactions; they have become the building blocks of a tight-knit community."

    Langston also thanked the employees, both past and present.

    "Each one of you has played an integral role in the success of our store, and I am profoundly grateful for your hard work, loyalty, and unwavering commitment. Your smiling faces, helpful advice, and camaraderie have created an atmosphere that made our store more than just a place to shop—it became a community hub and a second home to many."

    Langston didn't specify in the letter what led to the store's closure but mentioned that Two Rivers has "weathered countless storms" and faced challenges during its tenure.

    "Our journey has not been without its challenges, but it is through these challenges that we have emerged stronger and more resilient. It is the memories we have made and the bonds we have formed that will endure long after the store's doors have closed."

    Two Rivers was the only grocery store in Big Sandy, meaning shoppers will have to venture into neighboring towns and cities to buy groceries.

    Before the store officially closes, however, a huge sale is underway with 50 percent off all groceries and 25 percent off produce, meat, and dairy.

    Customers said they couldn't believe the store is closing for good.

    "It's the hub of the community," longtime shopper, Patti Rocell, told KETK.

    Rocell told the outlet she had been shopping at Two Rivers for 28 years and the store had been where many high school students had their first jobs.

    "They are always so friendly, neighborly and call you by name and it’s small town USA," she said.

    She hopes someone will buy the location and reopen a grocery store in the town.

    "Somebody needs to come and buy this store, buy all of its equipment inside, and reopen a grocery store in Big Sandy," she said.

    Langston ended the letter with one last heartfelt thank you for all the support the store has seen in the past 30 years.
