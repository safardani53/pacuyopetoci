    Bismack Biyombo | Career & Stats

    Unlike most other NBA players, Biyombo started his professional career in the Spanish ACB League with CB Illescas and stayed at the club for two years.

    Following impressive performances for the team, Bismack decided to move to Fuenlabrada, where he would attract interest from NBA scouts.

    Subsequently, the Congolese got invited to play in the 2011 Nike Hoops Summit, a competition where the top high school seniors from the U.S are pitted against top international teens.

    Other people would have been satisfied just with the call-up but not Biyombo.

    In a display of his raw talent, the future Hornets center recorded a triple-double with 10 blocks, 11 rebounds, and 12 points, playing against the likes of now-LA Lakers All-Star Anthony Davis.

    As a result, NBA general managers and scouts who came to watch the game were extremely impressed.

    Right after the game, Bismack stated,- “It gave me a chance to introduce myself to the NBA world and see if I could be drafted in the next year or two.”

    Biyombo’s dream came true in the subsequent 2011 NBA Draft as he was drafted 7th overall by the Sacramento Kings before trading the Congo native’s rights to Charlotte Bobcats (now Charlotte Hornets).

    Previously, when the Bobcats center was still a child back home in Congo, Biyombo did not have money to buy a pair of shoes.

    However, shoes were no longer a problem for young Bismack as he earned $2.7 million in his first year with the Charlotte-based franchise.

    After the draft, expectations were extremely high for the Congolese as he was a lottery pick.

    Unfortunately, the Bobcats center could never live up to the potential as he never averaged double figures in points, rebounds, and blocks.

    To illustrate, the best year of Bismack happened in the 2012-13 season, in which he managed an underwhelming 4.8 points, 1.8 blocks, and 7.3 rebounds.

    It was well below the expectations that were laid upon him at the beginning of his NBA career.

    Consequently, the Charlotte-based franchise declined the opportunity to extend Biyombo’s contract by one more year for the 2015-16 season, making him a free agent.

    Following his release from the Charlotte Bobcats, Biyombo said, – “In Charlotte, first you get the opportunity. But you have all this pressure. I didn’t have the opportunity to do what I needed to,”

    Soon after, the Toronto Raptors signed a two-year $6 million contract, with the second year being a player option.

    The 6 feet 8 center became a force in the franchise’s run to the Eastern Conference Finals as he became a shot-blocking and rebounding force with averages of 6.2 points, 1.4 blocks, and 9.4 rebounds during the playoffs.

    Subsequently, the Congo native declined his player option for the 2016-17 season since he was not the Raptors’ starting center.

    Contrarily, while the time with the Raptors was limited to just one season, Biyombo stated that he cherished the experience of reaching the Conference Finals and the city of Toronto.

    Having recently been impressed with the Raptors, many franchises were interested in Biyombo, but the Orlando Magic secured his services in the end.

    Likewise, Bismack signed the most lucrative deal of his career with the Hornets.

    Specifically, the Congo native signed a four-year $72 million contract with the Charlotte-based franchise.

    After that, the 6 feet 8 centers remained with the franchise for two years averaging 5.8 points, 1.1 blocks, and 6.4 rebounds per game.
