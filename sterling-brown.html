    Follow Up On The Incident

    After four months, the Milwaukee Police Department released a 30-minute video captured by a police body camera.

    Brown described the event as a “police intimidation effort followed by the unlawful use of physical force.”

    The City of Milwaukee Common Council authorized a $750k settlement with Sterling Brown for the 2018 encounter with police on May 4 by a 14–0 vote.

    Although there was no acknowledgment of constitutional rights breaches in the settlement, there was an apology and acceptance of an unwarranted escalation notwithstanding Mr. Brown’s calm demeanor.

    Officers participating in the activity have been suspended or moved, while others have been ordered to retrain.

    Furthermore, the agreement calls for a shift in police methods and establishes a set of anti-racist policing rules and procedures that penalize officers who violate civil and human rights.

    The settlement also includes a new requirement that cops report every instance in which an officer pulls his or her weapon.

    Rejection Of The Settlement

    Brown turned down a $400,000 settlement offer from the Milwaukee City Council in October 2019.

    The high settlement offer was a legal ploy designed to exploit Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 68.

    Sterling is obligated to pay the city’s legal fees and expenses under Rule 68 if he loses the lawsuit or receives damages of less than $400,000.

    Brown stated that he declined the offer in part because of his skills and sense of obligation to use his NBA platform to promote awareness.

    The player’s lawyer, Mark Thomsen, slammed the settlement offer, claiming it was the city’s attempt to save face rather than accept wrongdoing.
